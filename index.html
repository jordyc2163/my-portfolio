Why Web Development?

I chose web development for a few reasons. I wasn't sure initally what it entailed, only supported my best friend through his own journey as we finshed college. I first heard about it through his work, hearing him and our other friends talk about what they were working with or stuck on. It sounded mentally stimulating and being able to work from home or carry your work with you peaked my curiosity.
I always felt my career in nursing at the time was flexible with specific areas of interest to work in, such as L&D, OR, ER, anything that sparked your interest, for only 3 days a week. I found it not to be the case. I found myself drained by the end of the week, and much similarities between units that felt repetitive. I was interested in critical care thinking it would be mentally stimulating. I later realized I was getting less stimulated just as much as it was working my back. I also felt I was slowly burning out, feeling emotionally drained, and overwhelmed with poor staffing and patient ratios. With much thought, and discussions with my close friend group that are in the field, I think this career suits my ability to move around, ease the taxing demand on my body, and work my mind rather than to kill my back and sleep schedule by 30.
